Traditions of Republic Day

Republic Day is celebrated with great pomp and enthusiasm throughout India. The most iconic tradition is the parade held at Rajpath in New Delhi, which includes:

  • A display of India's defense capability with tanks, missiles, and aircraft.
  • March-past by the armed forces, police, and schoolchildren.
  • Cultural tableaux representing different states and union territories.

Fun Facts about Republic Day

  • The first Republic Day was celebrated on January 26, 1950, when Dr. Rajendra Prasad took the oath as the first President of India.
  • The Constitution of India is the longest written constitution in the world, with 448 articles in 22 parts and 12 schedules.
  • The grand parade at Rajpath takes months of preparation and involves thousands of participants.

Cultural Significance

Republic Day highlights India's cultural diversity and unity. The celebrations include performances by artists from various states, showcasing their traditional dances, music, and costumes. It is a day to celebrate the nation's heritage and values.
